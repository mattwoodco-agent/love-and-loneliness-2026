Latest Research Insights

Cornell University Study: "Why Feeling Alone May Matter More Than Being Alone"

A groundbreaking study published in JAMA Network Open introduces the concept of social asymmetry – the mismatch between objective social isolation and subjective loneliness feelings. Key findings:

  • People who feel lonelier than their social connections would predict face higher mortality risks
  • The subjective experience of loneliness may be more important than actual social isolation
  • This mismatch is linked to increased cardiovascular disease and chronic conditions

  • Genetic Factors: Some people may be predisposed to feeling lonely regardless of social circumstances
